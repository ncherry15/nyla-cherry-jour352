Black People Are Disproportionately Affected By the Prison System

Findings from Pew Research show racial disparities amongst incarcerated peoples.

By Nyla Cherry

Citizens who identify as Black are significantly more likely to be sent to jail. They are also significantly more likely to remain incarcerated for longer periods of time than their peers. These statistics are so alarming because Black people makeup such a small portion of Americans yet such a large portion of prison populations.
